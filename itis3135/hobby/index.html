Favorite Video Game Series

The Legend of Zelda

My all time favorite Zelda game, and possibly favorite game in general, it was also the first Zelda game I played. I got it back in 2010 and since then I have probably played through it over 20 times. Compared to the other Zelda games, this games darker themes and visuals stand out.

The Legend of Zelda, Skyward Sword is my second favorite Zelda game. This game utilized the Wii motion plus to track the Wii remote movement more accuratly and gives the game a more realistic feel in terms of combat.

The Legend of Zelda, Breath of the Wild is my third favorite game in the series. Unlike the other Zelda games, this one was the first to be open world. This game features a wide variety of puzzles, combat challenges and secrets all throughout a huge open world. It also looks great, despite the Switches underperformance.

Games I would Reccomend

Elden Ring

Elden Ring is a souls-like game from Fromsoft, the team that made Dark Souls. This game, like many others, was my first experience with a souls game. The thing about souls games is that they are meant to be pretty challenging, and can even feel unfair at times, but it is never impossible. It is an action rpg, with its main focus in combat. The enemies and bosses are difficult but it makes the game more exciting.

Crash Bandicoot

The first console games I ever played. Crash Bandicoot is a 3d platformer, originally released for the Playstion in 1996. Two more games were released after in the ps1 trilogy. I really like the movement in these games, its fast and responsive and rewards good gameplay. In the third game, they introduced a time trial mode that really takes the gameplay to a new level. In these, you will need to play close to perfect and optimize your movement to get the highest reward for the time trial.

Katamari Damacy

This game is a bit weird, and there isnt really anything else like it in terms of style and gameplay. The games story starts with the King of Cosmos accidently destroying the stars and planets. After awknoledging his mistake, he sends his 5cm tall son to earth with a magic ball called a katamari. Things smaller than the katamari will stick to it, and eventually cause it to grow in size. When they are big enough they are thrown into the sky and turned into planets and stars. A pretty relaxing game with lots of charm.
